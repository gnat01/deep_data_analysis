# Possible Questions

This file is a working bank of natural and analytical questions the YC / HN hiring-posts knowledge base should eventually answer well.

The point is coverage:

- direct factual lookup
- filtered retrieval
- time-series analysis
- company change analysis
- role / remote / compensation questions
- AI trend questions
- evidence-linked follow-up questions

## 40 Questions

1. In which months did DuckDuckGo post on HN hiring threads between January 2023 and April 2026?

2. Was there any month between December 2024 and January 2026 when DuckDuckGo was hiring for data science?

3. Which companies posted hiring ads in every month of 2025?

4. Which companies appeared for the first time in the HN hiring threads in 2026?

5. Which companies returned to hiring after being absent for at least 6 months?

6. Which month had the highest number of distinct companies posting?

7. Which month had the highest number of distinct roles posted?

8. How did the share of remote roles change from 2023 to 2026?

9. Which companies were remote-first in 2025?

10. Which companies shifted from remote to hybrid or onsite over time?

11. Which companies explicitly mentioned visa sponsorship?

12. Which companies mentioned compensation most consistently across their posts?

13. Which companies showed the widest variation in hiring-post language across time?

14. Which companies showed the least variation and mostly repeated the same post?

15. For a selected company, how did post semantic spread compare with role semantic spread over time?

16. Which companies had high role spread but low post spread?

17. Which companies had high post spread but relatively low role spread?

18. Which companies had the highest drift score over the full history?

19. Which companies materially changed their hiring narrative in 2025?

20. Which companies seemed to pivot and then return to an earlier hiring pattern?

21. Which role families grew the most between 2023 and 2026?

22. Which months saw the strongest increase in ML / AI roles?

23. Which companies were hiring for platform engineers in 2025?

24. Which companies were hiring for data engineers remotely in 2024?

25. Which companies were hiring for design roles while also mentioning AI concepts?

26. Which companies hired for both backend and ML roles in the same month?

27. Which companies posted the largest number of distinct role families in a single month?

28. What are the most common roles DuckDuckGo has hired for across all its posts?

29. For Apple, which months did it post, and what kinds of roles was it hiring for?

30. Which companies explicitly mentioned MCP, tool use, agents, evals, or RAG in 2026?

31. When did agent-related terminology start appearing meaningfully in the hiring corpus?

32. Which role families adopted agent / tooling language first?

33. Which companies mentioned GPT / LLM / generative AI in 2023?

34. Which companies changed from generic software hiring language to explicit AI-agent language over time?

35. Which product themes were most common in 2023 versus 2025 versus 2026 YTD?

36. Which companies appeared to change what they were building, based on theme shifts across posts?

37. Which companies repeatedly advertised the same compensation band month after month?

38. Which companies introduced compensation information later after initially omitting it?

39. Show me evidence rows for companies hiring for privacy, browser, or search-related roles.

40. For a chosen company, show the posts and roles that best explain why its change score is high or low.

41. Did any of the FAANG companies ever post on HN?

42. Did any companies change their compensation band for the same job role over time? If so, which ones?

43. In 50 words or less, how did the requirements for an AI engineer change from 2024 - 2026?

44. Which companies did not hire for any ML or AI roles for the entire duration of this dataset?

45. Give the geographical distribution of where companies were headquartered for every year.

46. What is the didtribution of global remote roles to overall remote roles across the dataset? Can you give this on a year by year basis?

47. Do Indian companies prefer onsite, hybrid or remote roles when they post?

48. What does the distribution of post lengths look like on a year by year basis?

49. Are there companies who wrote longer posts consistetly over the years, or is this not seen in the data?

50. Of the companies hiring remotely in 2025, which ones pay the same regardless of location or which ones give location adjusted compensation? 

## Why This Matters

This question bank should drive the next KB-helper layer.

If Task 3 can answer most of these cleanly, then the knowledge base is genuinely useful before we even add a natural-language layer on top.
